Major tech firms are investing $650B in AI infrastructure, but physical constraints like energy availability are becoming significant bottlenecks.

---

## 본문
As AI models scale to unprecedented sizes, the corresponding infrastructure demands have shattered historical records. The "Big Four" tech giants—Meta, Microsoft, Alphabet, and Amazon—are projected to spend a combined $650 billion on artificial intelligence infrastructure throughout 2026. 

However, capital expenditure is no longer the sole bottleneck. Physical constraints are now the primary limiting factors for AI advancement. Data center construction is increasingly delayed by grid capacity limitations and sheer energy availability. This reality is prompting tech giants to secure dedicated nuclear power agreements and invest heavily in next-generation cooling technologies. The infrastructure race has consequently shifted from simply purchasing GPUs to fundamentally reinventing global energy logistics.
