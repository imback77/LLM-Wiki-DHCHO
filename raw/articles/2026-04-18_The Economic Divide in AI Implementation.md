## 본문
The economic realities of enterprise AI adoption are crystallizing in 2026. Industry analysts are observing a "stark and widening divide" between companies effectively utilizing AI and those merely experimenting. 

A small, elite group of leading companies is currently capturing the vast majority of AI's economic and productivity value. These organizations have moved beyond using AI for basic efficiency gains; they are deploying it to fundamentally reinvent their business models, optimize global supply chains, and create novel product categories. Conversely, organizations bogged down in prolonged pilot phases are beginning to see negative ROI as deployment costs outpace their modest productivity improvements. This dichotomy is reshaping competitive advantages across virtually all market sectors.
